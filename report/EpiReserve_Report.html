EpiReserve - SIR Epidemic Mortality and Insurance Reserve Stress

1. Introduction

This project models the impact of an epidemic on life insurance reserves using the SIR (Susceptible-Infected-Recovered) compartmental model. During pandemics like COVID-19, insurers face unexpected excess mortality that can dramatically increase claims. This analysis quantifies that impact and calculates the additional reserves required.

2. The SIR Model

The SIR model divides a population of N individuals into three compartments:

  • S(t): Susceptible — healthy individuals who could get infected
  • I(t): Infected — currently sick and contagious individuals
  • R(t): Recovered/Removed — recovered or deceased individuals

The system is governed by three ordinary differential equations:

\[\frac{dS}{dt} = -aSI\] \[\frac{dI}{dt} = aSI - bI\] \[\frac{dR}{dt} = bI\]

where \(a\) is the infection rate and \(b\) is the recovery rate.

The basic reproduction number \(R_0 = \frac{aN}{b}\) determines epidemic behavior:

  • \(R_0 > 1\): epidemic spreads
  • \(R_0 < 1\): epidemic dies out

Parameters were chosen to reflect a COVID-19 like scenario with \(R_0 = 3\).

3. Epidemic Simulation

With \(R_0 = 3\), the epidemic peaks around day 60 and infects approximately 940,000 out of 1,000,000 individuals. Around 59,500 people are never infected — this occurs because as S decreases, the effective reproduction number \(\frac{aS}{b}\) drops below 1 and the epidemic loses momentum.

4. Mortality Analysis

With a Case Fatality Rate of 1%, the epidemic produces:

  • Total epidemic deaths: 9405
  • Peak daily deaths: 300
  • Peak death day: 74

5. Actuarial Reserve Impact

Scenario Deaths Total Claims
Baseline (no epidemic) 40 € 4e+06
Epidemic stressed 9445 € 944,457,720

Key findings:

  • Extra claims from epidemic: € 940,457,720
  • Reserve stress ratio: 236.11x
  • Extra reserve per policy: € 94,046

The reserve stress ratio of 236.11x means the insurer would need 236.11 times their normal reserve to cover claims during an epidemic of this severity.

6. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that even a moderate epidemic with \(R_0 = 3\) can create reserve stress ratios exceeding 200x for a life insurance portfolio. Real actuaries would extend this model by:

  • Using age-stratified mortality rates from DAV life tables
  • Modelling partial immunity and vaccination effects
  • Running sensitivity analysis across multiple R0 and CFR scenarios
  • Applying regulatory stress tests as required by Solvency II
